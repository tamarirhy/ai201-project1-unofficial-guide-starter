I'll start by saying this is a review from a parent.

Location -- It's pretty good. Uhouse is in the heart of midtown Atlanta and across from a grocery store, walking distance to campus, shopping, bars, restaurants, and a strip club that is known nationally. It's not the safest area late at night. If you have a room up high, you'll get a beautiful view. (Although the west side will get the sun.)

Staff -- The sales staff is responsive when you call, but it is difficult to get in touch with anyone on the management-side. And, they have little to no flexibility in helping residents aside from the cosmetics of the facility. They were less than helpful when it came to dealing with some more substantial issues.

Lease -- Once you sign their contract, you're locked in for a year. Forget any hardships, medical leave, etc. I'm guessing if a student passed away, the estate would still be obligated to pay rent until it ends. They also seek out a commitment early on for the following year as well. You can transfer the lease, but they'll nickel and dime you on fees there too.

Amenities -- Impressive, until you realize that you have the same on campus, minus a washer and dryer for most rooms. The Stinger shuttle runs by the complex.

Parking -- Available, but costly

Rooms -- Different floorplans. Shared kitchen and living area. Washer and dryer included. Bedrooms are private entry. Each room also has its bathroom.

Uhouse was built in 2015 and is showing it's age prematurely. It was a disappointing experience. There are plenty of options in midtown and even on campus. Research your options before committing to Uhouse.
